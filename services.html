01 — Governance

Governance & Academic Boards.

For Councils, Academic Boards, and the Chairs who run them.

What iMORSE does here

Independent review of board and Academic Board effectiveness. Composition, meeting cadence, paper quality, committee structure, and the relationship between Council and Academic Board. Where appropriate, iMORSE takes Chair or Deputy Chair appointments directly.

  • Academic Board and Council effectiveness reviews
  • Committee architecture and terms of reference
  • Chair and Deputy Chair appointments (current and recent)
  • Board paper quality and decision-tracking
  • Governance remediation following external audit findings
Recent work. Chair of the Academic Board at Torrens University. Deputy Chair at ACAP University College. Academic Board positions at LCI Melbourne, CG Spectrum Institute and VIT.
02 — Strategy

Strategy & planning.

Institutional strategy, research strategy, and the choices under the mission statement.

What iMORSE does here

Board-ready strategy and planning advice. The work begins with the data — demand, ranking position, cost base, research concentration — and ends with a small number of defended choices. Options are stated. Trade-offs are named. Recommendations are singular.

  • Institutional strategy and planning cycles
  • Research strategy, concentration and investment
  • International branch-campus and joint-venture strategy
  • Merger, acquisition and partnership due diligence
  • AAT expert-witness testimony on university standards
Recent work. BCG-led engagement with a Saudi university (2026). Branch-campus and Mandarin-delivery due diligence for Australian universities. Research strategy advice drawing on fifteen years as a DVC and Dean.

Regulation & quality assurance.

For providers working with TEQSA, and for international analogues.

What iMORSE does here

Registration, re-registration and Threshold Standards work for Australian public and private providers. Cyclic review, academic integrity frameworks, and the internal audit scaffolding that makes external audits survivable. International equivalents where applicable.

  • TEQSA registration and re-registration support
  • Threshold Standards gap analysis and remediation
  • Cyclic review and self-accreditation frameworks
  • Academic integrity and contract-cheating policy
  • Acting as a TEQSA auditor (independent panel work)
Relevant background. Current TEQSA auditor. Led the first Australian academic unit to ISO 9001 accreditation at Monash. Home Affairs submission on higher education, research and regulation (2021).
04 — Analytics

Analytics & modelling.

Quantitative decision support, delivered by someone who can explain the model to the Chair.

What iMORSE does here

Ranking, demand and risk modelling. Scenario analysis for Council. Performance dashboards built for governance, not for external communications. The underlying methods are disclosed. The assumptions are named. A board member without a quantitative background can follow the argument.

  • Ranking-position modelling and scenario analysis
  • Enrolment demand, retention and completion modelling
  • Financial and operational risk modelling
  • KPI frameworks designed for board review
  • Independent model validation and method review
Relevant background. PhD in Operations Research (Warwick). Fellow of the Operational Research Society and the Australian Computer Society. Chair of the Board of the Analytics Institute of Australia. Earlier career in financial and business modelling at Deloitte and PwC.

How engagements are structured.

A scope, a fee, a timeline, and a written deliverable. Nothing unusual.

iMORSE does not take retainers. Each engagement is a defined piece of work with a named output. Where continuity matters — a board seat, an ongoing advisory role — it is structured explicitly, not accreted quietly.

  • 01Scoping call. A free conversation to confirm the question, the audience for the output, and whether iMORSE is the right fit.
  • 02Written proposal. Scope, method, fee, timeline, and deliverables. No fine print. If the answer is a board paper, the proposal says that.
  • 03Senior delivery. All substantive work — interviews, analysis, drafting — is done by Professor Flitman.
  • 04Draft and review. Drafts are shared early. Factual corrections welcomed. Conclusions iMORSE stands behind are iMORSE's to defend.
  • 05Final paper. One or two-page executive summary, findings, options, a recommendation, the next step. Confidential.

Where iMORSE is not the right fit.

Three cases, for the record.

  • —Large-team delivery. iMORSE is a single-practitioner firm. Work that genuinely needs a ten-person team should go to a large-firm partner, and iMORSE will say so.
  • —Predetermined conclusions. iMORSE does not put its name to a finding it does not believe. Engagements where the conclusion is fixed before the work begins are not a fit.
  • —Public-relations support. iMORSE does not do communications strategy, media positioning, or thought-leadership writing. The work is governance, strategy, regulation and analytics — nothing adjacent.

If the question fits, a short call will confirm it.

Scoping calls are free. Proposals are written. Decisions can wait until you have read one.
